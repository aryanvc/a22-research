# Subsea Cloud

**Status: Zombie — alive in marketing, zero independently verified deployments in ~4.5 years.** The segment's cautionary tale on announcement-vs-reality.

## What they claimed to build

Founded late 2021 by Maxie Reynolds (ex-offshore energy tooling, ex-cybersecurity). The technical differentiator vs. Microsoft Natick: **unpressurized, pressure-equalized pods** — interior dielectric immersion fluid matched to seawater pressure instead of an expensive pressure vessel. Claims: cheap containers usable to 3,000 m depth, ~$600K per MW deployed vs. ~$6M on land, 40% power/CO2 reduction, 12–16 weeks contract-to-live, "underwater landlord" colo model targeting healthcare, finance, military, AI, gaming.

## The announcement record

Three named projects announced 2022, none ever confirmed in the water:

- **Jules Verne** — Port Angeles, WA. 20-ft container, ~800 servers, ~1 MW at 9 m depth. "Online by end of 2022"; secondary sources say hardware was still in testing mid-2023. No independent confirmation it was ever submerged or served a customer.
- **Njord01** — Gulf of Mexico, 213–274 m. Never confirmed deployed.
- **Manannan** — North Sea, ~183–213 m. Never confirmed deployed.

Further unverified claims: "already operates 13,500 servers in underwater locations in Asia" (no location, customer, or photo ever produced); pods in Scottish/Norwegian waters; a prototype in India; 90-day customer trials off Norway; a 3 MW Danish "North Sea Super AI Cluster" slated for 2025; Singapore ambitions. Every deployment claim traces back to the company itself.

## Funding and customers

No disclosed funding rounds, no named investors, no named customers — ever.

## Status 2026

Website active and now pitching AI-ready pods "up to 700 MW per site" co-located with offshore wind/nuclear — with zero named deployments or case studies, and a 404ing about page. No regulatory fights on record (nothing to permit if nothing goes in the water).

## Lesson for the segment

The unpressurized-pod idea is a genuine cost insight (Natick's pressure vessel was the expensive part), but this company is the benchmark for how far pure announcements travel in this space. Diligence rule the space teaches: **no photo of a pod in the water + no named customer = it didn't happen.**
